About the class

A gentle step into a bigger world.

Junior Preschool is where children build their first real friendships. The day centres on social relationships, language, following routines, growing independence, and learning empathy toward others — all through play-based discovery, with 45 minutes of outdoor playground time and daily creative activities. Toilet training isn’t required, and children may join from our Toddler class once they’re ready.

A day in Junior Preschool

The rhythm of the day.

Predictable, unhurried, and full of choice — the structure that helps little ones feel secure. And new this September: the day can continue through lunch and the afternoon, until 2:45.

What a week looks like

Play with a purpose, every single day.

Our educators plan fresh activities each week across nine areas of the room — here’s the kind of thing your child will dive into.

📚

Language & Literacy

Magnetic letters, name tracing, puppet-theatre storytelling, felt-board rhymes and I-Spy books.

🧩

Cognitive & Problem-Solving

Puzzles, gear boards, colour-sorting with tongs, magnetic mazes and lockboxes.

🎨

Art Studio

Playdough sculpting, seasonal stamping, tissue-paper collage, easel painting and do-a-dot.

💧

Sensory Play

Water wheels & test tubes, bug investigation with magnifying glasses, rice & bean bins, floating flowers.

⚽

Gross Motor & Sportball

Sportball, obstacle courses, balance beams, hockey sticks & scoops, ribbons and parachute.

🌱

Outdoor & Garden

Planting and watering the garden, mud kitchen, egg hunts, treasure hunts, trikes & scooters.

🎭

Dramatic Play

Space station, doctor’s office, grocery store, diner and construction site role-play.

🔬

Science & Nature

Life cycles of frogs, butterflies & bees, light-table discovery, and five-senses exploration.

🎵

Music & Circle

Theme books, songs & fingerplays, group games, calendar & weather, and table work.

Grounded in ELECT — every activity maps to Ontario’s Early Learning framework, so play is always building real skills: counting, phonological awareness, fine motor control, self-regulation and more.

Good to know

Junior Preschool questions, answered.

Does my child need to be toilet trained?

No. Toilet training isn’t required for any class. We work alongside you to encourage toileting readiness as a positive, pressure-free experience.

What is a co-operative nursery school?

A co-operative is a non-profit run with its families. Parents take part in classroom life and in how the school is run, which keeps classes small, fees lower, and the community close. We’ll explain the options on your tour — or see how the co-op works and the volunteer roles.

What’s your teaching approach?

Learning through play, guided by ELECT — Ontario’s Early Learning framework. Our RECE educators build the program around the children’s real interests, so play is always developing language, early numeracy, motor skills and social-emotional growth.

How big are the classes, and what are the ratios?

Junior Preschool stays small, with high adult-to-child ratios and at least one Registered Early Childhood Educator (RECE) in every classroom.

How does the move up from the Toddler class work?

Children transition from Toddler to Junior around 2.3–2.6 years, once they’re comfortably navigating stairs and the playground independently — always at a pace that’s right for your child.

Do you take part in the CWELCC government program?

Not at this time. Our board found that staying outside CWELCC protects our program quality, staffing flexibility and financial stability as a small co-op.
