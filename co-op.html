The co-operative difference · since 1948

A school we run together.

Oriole is a non-profit co-operative: our classes are led by professional educators, and the school is guided by a board of directors made up of parents. Every family lends a hand — that’s what keeps our classes small, our fees lower, and our community close.

How it works

Two ways to be part of the co-op.

When you enrol, you choose the level of classroom involvement that suits your family. Either way, every family holds one volunteer role.

Participating

Duty Days, for lower tuition

A parent or caregiver assists in the classroom on a Duty Day once every two weeks per child, in exchange for reduced tuition. It’s a lovely window into your child’s school life.

Can’t make a Duty Day? You can hire an assistant for the day or swap with another family.

Non-participating

No Duty Days

Prefer not to assist in the classroom? Non-participating tuition covers a hired classroom assistant instead — no classroom assistance requirement at all.

Duty Days cover the morning program only (9:00 a.m.–12:00 p.m.) — even if your child stays for the new extended day. Afternoon staffing is handled by our educators.
Volunteer roles

One small role per child — most take about an hour a week.

Each September, families send us their top three choices and we match everyone to a role. Most are done from home on your own schedule; a few involve time at the school.

Roles and counts are from the current school year and vary a little from year to year, based on enrolment.

Board of Directors

Help steer the school.

Oriole is governed by an elected parent Board that meets about once a month in the evenings. Board work is mostly done from home, the time commitment is about the same as a non-Board role — and it’s a great way to meet new families and have a real say in the direction of the school.

President

Chair our monthly Board meetings and act as the link between the Board, our Supervisor and staff — helping set direction and keep the school running smoothly.

~1 hr/weekFrom home

Vice President

Support the President, help guide Board decisions, and step in to chair meetings when needed.

~1 hr/weekFrom home

Secretary

Take minutes at Board meetings and circulate agendas, minutes and records to keep everyone on the same page.

~1 hr/weekFrom home

Treasurer

Oversee the school’s finances alongside our administrator — budgets, the annual fee schedule and a short financial report for each Board meeting. Comfortable with Excel and basic bookkeeping.

~1 hr/weekFrom home

Fundraising Director

Set the year’s fundraising goals and lead the committee planning our biggest event of the year — Oriole’s silent-auction Gala.

Heavier before the Gala

Registrar

Welcome prospective families — conduct tours on your own schedule, report on admissions at Board meetings, and help review the admissions process each year.

~1 hr/week
